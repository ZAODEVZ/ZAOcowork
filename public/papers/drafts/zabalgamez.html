ZABAL Games - Whitepaper (draft v0.2)

ZABAL Games

The ZAO's three-month Farcaster-native builder onboarding event. Free to join. Ship something real in public with a ZAO mentor embedded as your teammate. Earn Respect (the ZAO's soulbound merit currency), get an on-chain collectible, and build for a real 100+ member community.

Why it matters

The problem: builders stay isolated

Builders want to ship fast and get real feedback. Existing hackathons are one-weekend vanity events with no lasting community. Traditional grants are slow and solo. ZABAL Games solves this: it's a structured, recurring event where builders come in, work alongside mentors from The ZAO, ship real artifacts for a real ecosystem, and join a community that keeps compounding.

The ZAO is a decentralized impact network built on contribution, not tokens. When you build at ZABAL Games, your work is measured by Respect (soulbound, earned through the Fractal governance system) and validated by peers. You get a Hats Protocol credential proving you shipped here. More importantly, you join a network of creators building music, governance, and tooling infrastructure for the open web.

What it is

Three tracks, three months, real stakes

ZABAL Games is a free, Farcaster-native onboarding event for The ZAO ecosystem. Anyone is welcome. Three parallel tracks let builders with different strengths step in together:

  • Artist track Musical and visual builds - instruments, visuals, interactive art, music tooling.
  • Builder track Developer and aspiring-coder builds - smart contracts, agents, dApps, infrastructure.
  • Creator track Media, distribution, and content builds - documentation, streams, education, curation.

The audience is the ZAO community itself - over 100 active members shipping Respect weekly. Your artifact lands in front of people who understand the stakes and can actually use what you built. Not a leaderboard you forget.

Timeline

June: workshops | July: open build | August: Finals

June - Prep & workshops

Recorded Far-Hack-style sessions covering ZAO governance, Respect and the Fractal system, ZOLs (contribution credits), and the ecosystem tools. Instructors: ZAO teachers and ecosystem partners including Empire Builder team, Bonfire (knowledge graph), POIDH (prediction market bounties), Juke (music tooling), and SongJam (music tokenization). Watchable live or async at zabalgamez.com/recordings. Topics rotate: first half workshop-style (deep dives), second half panel-style (Q&A with tool builders).

July - Open build-a-thon

Public submission window (Month 2 of the event). Goal for 2026: 200 distinct builders, multiple submissions per person. The bar is concrete: live URL + open-source GitHub repo + 60-second demo video + a cast to the /zabal Farcaster channel. Partial and work-in-progress drafts count - there is no perfection penalty. Every submission that clears the bar earns Respect to the builder(s). Mentors watch rolling submissions, offer feedback, and begin mentor-builder pair formation.

August - Finals (72-hour marathon)

Mentor-and-builder pairs lock. All finalists receive a shared prompt and a 72-hour window split into three phases: 24h build (mentor embedded as teammate), 24h promote (ZAO amplifies all finalist builds), 24h vote (ZAO members vote via Fractal governance, 1-person-1-vote). A live reveal stream closes the event. Every finalist is paid in USDC on a tiered scale; additionally, all finalists (July builders who earned Respect + August finalists) receive a Hats Protocol role NFT on Base as a universal "I shipped at ZABAL Games" credential.

Mechanics

How to submit and build

Entry

Register on the site (/enter) with a wallet and a GitHub repo. Signups and collectible issuance run through Magnetiq (app.magnetiq.xyz), the all-in-one event platform.

Mentors

Mentors are an open, public roster - not a fixed selection committee. Each mentor defines the builder archetype they want to work with and pairs with an open-submission builder from the cohort. Mentors receive a Hats Protocol "ZAO Mentor" role on Base. This is a signal of trust and reputation within The ZAO.

Submissions

July builds are tracked on a public GitHub-backed board. Bonfire (a distributed knowledge graph at bonfires.ai) serves as the system of record - every submission is indexed and queryable, both by agents (for automated judging) and by humans (for browsing). The Bonfire integration lets builders push progress updates to GitHub and have them automatically surfaced in a knowledge-graph layer that judges can reason over. This moves the evaluation from "best guess" to "evidence-based."

Optional tools

Empire Builder (leaderboard and token infrastructure) and Clanker (token launcher) are available but entirely optional. Builders can prototype mechanics in a tokenless Empire first, then add token rewards later if it makes sense. This is the best practice: ship the game mechanics first, add economic incentives after you know what you're building.

The submission bar

Live URL + open-source repo + 60s demo + cast on /zabal. That's it. No deck required, no video production, no marketing. Just proof you shipped something real in public.

Rewards & recognition

Respect, credentials, and USDC

Respect

Every July builder who clears the submission bar earns Respect - The ZAO's soulbound contribution currency. Respect is non-tradable; it represents merit and governance weight. Accumulated Respect gives you voting power in ZAO decisions. The system runs on The Fractal (an on-chain governance mechanism adapted from Eden DAO), where contributions are voted on asynchronously, weekly, and ranked by peer consensus. More Respect = stronger voice in what The ZAO builds next.

NFT credential

All finalists (July builders + August Finals participants) receive a Hats Protocol collectible on Base. This is a universal "I shipped at ZABAL Games" credential - verifiable on-chain, non-transferable (soulbound), and recognized across the Web3 ecosystem. Builders can add it to their portfolio, use it in future grant applications, or just keep it as proof they shipped.

August Finals prizes

Finalists compete for a USDC prize pool distributed on a tiered scale. Example distribution (assuming 8 finalists): 1st place $150, 2nd $100, 3rd $75, 4th-8th $35 each. Every finalist walks away paid. Voting is Respect-weighted, ensuring that ZAO contributors who've proven themselves have more say in who wins.

Ecosystem

Partnerships and integrations

Magnetiq

All-in-one event platform (magnetiq.io, founded by Tyler Stambaugh). Handles ZABAL Games signup flow, collectible issuance, and the brand portal. Builders register here; mentor matching is coordinated through the same interface.

Bonfire

Distributed knowledge graph (bonfires.ai). Surfaces every build as a queryable, ranked artifact. Agents can use Bonfire to evaluate submissions against a rubric. Builders can update Bonfire via GitHub commits, creating a live record of progress from "initial commit" through "ship."

Empire Builder

Leaderboard and on-chain incentive infrastructure. Builders can use Empire to track scores, distribute rewards, and even tokenize their game. Not required - many July submissions work fine without it.

Clanker

Token launcher on Base. Builders can use Clanker to launch a reward token for their game if they want. Example: a ZABAL Games finalist launches a token for their music game and does an airdrop to ZABAL Games supporters.

Cal.com

Open-source Calendly alternative. Hosts the ZABAL Games workshop slot-booking system at cal.com/bettercallzaal/zabal-games-workshop-slot. Builders can sign up for 1-on-1 mentorship slots during June.

Restream

Stream-to-multiple-platforms tool (restream.io). ZABAL Games workshops and Finals stream simultaneously to multiple platforms (Twitch, YouTube, Farcaster, etc.) via Restream.

Lu.ma

Event platform. ZAO calendar and workshop scheduling via luma.com/zao. Used for calendar integration and reminder emails.

Role in The ZAO

Front door for builders, parallel to WaveWarZ

The ZAO has two front doors: ZABAL Games (for builders) and WaveWarZ (for players and music fans). Both funnel into the same community. Fans arrive at WaveWarZ, play, and become contributors with Respect. Builders arrive at ZABAL Games, ship, and become contributors with Respect. Both paths are equally valued. The goal is a self-reinforcing network: more contributors = better tooling = better games = more players = more contributors.

ZABAL Games specifically onboards technical builders into an ecosystem that values open source, governance, and transparent contribution. The June workshops teach Respect and Fractal governance explicitly, so builders understand they're not just winning a prize - they're joining a DAO-like structure with real decision-making power.

Open questions

Honest unknowns

Will 200 distinct July builders actually materialize? July is early (as of July 9, we're just past the first week). The bar is low (URL + repo + demo + cast), so friction should be minimal, but community size is still growing.

How will Bonfire judging actually work in practice? The theory is sound - agents can rank submissions against a rubric using the knowledge graph. The test comes in August when finalists are voted on. If the Bonfire integration works as designed, voting becomes evidence-based rather than gut-feel.

Can tokenless Empire Builder prototyping sustain engagement? The pattern (tokenless first, add token later) is new. If builders feel like they're "missing out" by skipping the token layer during July, they might add unnecessary complexity. August will reveal whether this is the right order.

How to participate

Get involved

As a builder: Head to zabalgamez.com/enter, connect your wallet and GitHub, and explore the three tracks. Attend June workshops at zabalgamez.com/recordings to learn the stack and understand Respect/Fractal. Ship a build in July. The bar is concrete - you've got this.

As a mentor: Register your interest on Magnetiq. Define the builder archetype you want to work with. You'll be matched with a July submission and embedded as a teammate for the August Finals sprint. You'll receive a Hats Protocol mentor role on Base.

As a contributor: Join the Farcaster /zabal channel. Vote on finalists in August. You'll earn Respect for constructive participation. Become a part of The ZAO's governance layer.

As a tool builder: If you maintain something that could integrate with ZABAL Games (leaderboards, knowledge graphs, token launchers, streaming overlays), reach out to Zaal on Farcaster (FID 19640, @bettercallzaal). Empire Builder, Clanker, Bonfire, and Restream all started as third-party partnerships.
