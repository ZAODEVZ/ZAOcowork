Zuke - Whitepaper (draft v0.2)

Zuke

The ZAO's audio-spaces app - live audio rooms where the community talks, wired into the content pipeline. Where ephemeral moments become permanent assets.

1. Problem & Context

Why live audio matters (and why it disappears)

In most platforms, live audio conversations evaporate the moment the room closes. On Farcaster (the ZAO's home), live audio is where the community actually forms - unscripted, immediate, full-bandwidth human connection. But that signal gets lost. There's no recording. No way to remix it. No way to reward the artists who created it. No distribution layer to extend its reach.

The ZAO needs a way to keep those moments, route them into production, and make them valuable. That is Zuke.

2. What It Is

Architecture & Tech Stack

Zuke is a Next.js application (repo: ZAODEVZ/Zuke) that wraps and extends Juke - nickysap's Farcaster-native live audio application (juke.audio). It runs at zuke.thezao.com.

Core dependencies

  • Juke - The underlying live audio room engine. Uses LiveKit for the audio transport layer and SIWF (Sign In With Farcaster) for authentication. Created and maintained by nickysap.
  • Zaal - The ZAO community host who operates and schedules Zuke spaces on behalf of The ZAO.
  • LiveKit - WebRTC-based audio infrastructure powering the actual room.
  • Next.js - Web framework running Zuke's UI, space management, and recording orchestration.
  • SIWF - Authentication layer, allowing hosts and participants to sign in with their Farcaster identity.
3. How It Works

The Recording & Processing Pipeline

Zuke's core job is to capture live audio and route it into the ZAO's content machinery. The flow:

Phase 1: Room Management

Zaal (or an authorized ZAO operator) creates a live space directly in Zuke. Zuke provisions a Juke room, opens it for participants, and streams the audio through LiveKit. Participants authenticate with SIWF and join the room. The audio is continuously recorded for the duration of the session.

Phase 2: Post-Production

When the space closes, Zuke's pipeline kicks in:

  • Import & Durability - Zuke can also import existing X Space recordings and treat them identically to native Juke recordings. This ensures durability: a conversation that happened elsewhere can be captured and put through the same machinery.
  • Recap & Snippet Generation - The system generates textual recaps and identifies highlight clips from the raw recording.
  • Space Lifecycle - Zuke has API-level hooks to create new spaces and end existing ones, allowing automated orchestration of the room cycle.
  • Transport Evolution - Currently, Zuke uses 100ms as part of its recording stack. A multi-provider path is in active development, moving toward a cleaner abstraction (harmonized with HMS - Huddl Media Services). This keeps Zuke insulated from future changes in any single provider.

Phase 3: Distribution

Once recorded and processed, Zuke recordings become the headwater of the ZAO content pipeline. They feed:

  • ZAOVideoEditor - Transforms space recordings into YouTube-ready long-form videos with editing, graphics, and proper metadata.
  • Clip Pipeline (Research doc 992) - Extracts highlight moments from recordings and turns them into short-form clips, each with a POIDH clip bounty. This rewards creators for their moments.
One live space becomes multiple assets: a full YouTube video, a set of short clips, and a batch of bounties paid to artists who created standout moments.
4. Why It Matters

The Connection to The ZAO Flywheel

Zuke sits at a critical junction in The ZAO ecosystem. It is not primarily a social app or a performance venue. It is production infrastructure - the capture point for the community's unscripted creative energy.

Without Zuke, live audio is ephemeral and unrewarded. With it, every space creates:

  • Permanent Record - The conversation exists forever, searchable and shareable.
  • Attribution - Speakers and artists are named and tied to their moments.
  • Monetization Path - Recaps and clips become bounties. Short-form content drives distribution. YouTube videos drive awareness.
  • Network Effect - More people participate in live spaces because they know they'll reach a broader audience downstream. This accelerates ZAO community growth.
  • Content Library - The ZAO builds a durable archive of its culture and knowledge, available for remixing, education, and reference.

Zuke makes The ZAO's live audio scalable and economically productive.

6. Distribution & Partners

Integrations & Connections

Zuke does not exist in isolation. It is wired into the broader ZAO content ecosystem.

Upstream: Juke (Farcaster)

Juke.audio hosts the actual live rooms. Zuke wraps the Juke experience and adds recording, recap generation, and downstream routing. The relationship is: Juke provides the room; Zuke provides the durability and production layer.

Downstream: ZAOVideoEditor

Zuke recordings are fed into ZAOVideoEditor, which transforms them into polished, long-form YouTube content. This is where Zuke recordings reach the broadest audience.

Downstream: Clip Pipeline (Doc 992)

Short-form clips are extracted from Zuke recordings via the clip pipeline. Each clip is attached to a POIDH bounty, incentivizing the identification and extraction of high-value moments. This turns ephemeral comments into rewarded contributions.

Hosting

Live at zuke.thezao.com. Built on Next.js and deployed via Vercel (vercel.app). Repository at github.com/ZAODEVZ/Zuke.

7. Current Status & Roadmap

Where Zuke Is Now & What's Next

Shipped & Active

As of July 2026, Zuke is live and in active use by The ZAO community. The core capabilities are in production:

  • Zuke Spaces - Live audio rooms, fully integrated with Juke and recording on all sessions.
  • Recording Pipeline - Captures all live conversations and stores them for downstream processing.
  • Recap Generation - Automatic generation of text summaries from recorded spaces.
  • Clip Extraction - Identification of high-value moments within recordings.

In Progress

Multi-provider transport abstraction - moving from 100ms toward a cleaner seam that supports HMS (Huddl Media Services) and other providers. This makes the system more resilient and decouples Zuke from any single vendor.

Known Open Questions

The following remain unsolved or partially solved:

  • Scaling - As ZAO spaces grow in frequency and size, Zuke's recording and processing infrastructure must scale. The multi-provider work is partly driven by this need.
  • Moderation - Who moderates live spaces? What are the rules? How are abuse and harassment handled? These policies are in formation.
  • Monetization Timing - POIDH clip bounties are the primary monetization path, but the timing, sizing, and discovery of those bounties is still being optimized.
  • Attribution - Ensuring that all speakers and contributors to moments in recordings are properly credited and can be rewarded is still a work in progress.
8. How to Participate

Getting Involved

I want to host a ZAO space in Zuke. What do I do?
Connect with Zaal (the ZAO space host) or reach out through The ZAO community channels. Zaal manages scheduling and host access for Zuke spaces.
I found a great moment in a recording. Can I clip it?
Yes. The clip pipeline is designed to identify and extract highlight moments from Zuke recordings. Clipped moments become POIDH bounties, and the creator of the clip (and the featured speakers) are rewarded.
Can I access the Zuke codebase?
Yes. The repo is public at ZAODEVZ/Zuke. Contributions and feedback are welcome.
How do I report a bug or suggest a feature?
File an issue on the GitHub repository or reach out to The ZAO engineering team through official channels.
