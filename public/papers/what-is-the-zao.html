01 - What is The ZAO?

The ZAO is a decentralized impact network that brings profit margin, data, and IP rights back to artists using blockchain and AI. Its first artist domain is music - not the only thing it will ever touch, but where it proves the model first.

The priority stack, in order, is music first, community second, technology third. Technology is the mechanism that makes the return of profit, data, and rights possible; it is not the headline, and it is never the point on its own.

02 - What does ZAO stand for?

ZTalent Artist Organization

That is the literal acronym. The working descriptor for what it is today is a decentralized impact network - use the acronym for the name, and the impact-network framing for what it actually does day to day.

03 - Who founded The ZAO?

Zaal Panthaki (BetterCallZaal)

Zaal Panthaki, also known as BetterCallZaal, founded The ZAO and leads the ecosystem across music, community, and technology. Per the honest-part section of the main Whitepaper, he currently carries most of the operating capital while the network works toward self-sustaining funding through tools, events, grants, and partners[1].

04 - Is The ZAO a record label?

No - not a label, not just a music community

The ZAO is explicitly not a record label and not merely a music community. It is an impact network whose first artist domain happens to be music. Where a label takes ownership of an artist's work, The ZAO returns three things to them instead: profit margin, data, and IP rights. Technology is the means of returning those things, not the marketing headline.

05 - What does The ZAO return to artists?

Profit margin, data, and IP rights

The mission is to move those three things from intermediaries back to the artists who create the value, using blockchain and AI as the mechanism rather than the pitch. WaveWarZ is the clearest running proof: artists are paid instantly, and the large majority of every dollar traded stays in the ecosystem rather than going to a middleman[2].

06 - How does governance work?

The Fractal and Respect

Governance runs on the Fractal, and its unit of account is Respect: an on-chain contribution currency - a soulbound OG ERC-20 plus a ZOR ERC-1155, both on Optimism[3]. A weekly Respect Game ranks contributions, OREC (optimistic execution with commitment-and-reveal) settles the outcomes, and a Fibonacci curve shapes the rewards.

As of 2026-07-10 there were 122 Respect (OG) holders on Optimism. Cumulative holdings are concentrated - the top 5 holders own about 34% of supply, the top 10 about 53%[4]. That is a different number from the per-round Fibonacci curve's own Gini (~0.23), which measures how evenly a single week's reward pool splits by rank, not how concentrated all-time holdings are[5]. A fair weekly curve still produces unequal cumulative holdings over 100+ unbroken weeks, since early and consistent contributors compound their share round over round - see the Technical Whitepaper for the full breakdown. Source: Optimism Etherscan, OG Respect contract, live-checked 2026-07-10
07 - What is Respect?

The on-chain contribution currency

Respect is a soulbound reputation token (an OG ERC-20 plus a ZOR ERC-1155) on Optimism. It is earned by contributing, ranked weekly in the Respect Game, and shaped by a Fibonacci reward curve. Because it is soulbound, it cannot be bought, sold, or transferred - only earned[3].

08 - What is the Fractal?

The governance process

Contributions are ranked in a weekly Respect Game, outcomes settle through OREC, and rewards follow a Fibonacci curve. It is how a decentralized network of artists and builders coordinates without a traditional top-down label structure - contribution over capital, not money buying influence[1].

09 - What does The ZAO build?

Four production lanes

WaveWarZ

Live-traded music battles in a prediction-market format - the front door to the ecosystem for many new participants.

ZABAL Games

A three-month build-a-thon that runs builders through workshops and shipping sprints.

The ZAO festivals

Live events under a festivals umbrella - ZAOstock, ZAOville, ZAO-PALOOZA, ZAO-CHELLA.

ZAO OS

The lab and monorepo where new ZAO things are prototyped before they graduate to their own homes.

Several other brands live inside the ecosystem beyond these four flagship lanes (SongJam, FISHBOWLZ, the ZABAL token, ZOUNZ governance, and more) - see the full papers index for their individual drafts.

10 - What is WaveWarZ?

Live-traded music battles

WaveWarZ is one of The ZAO's production lanes: music competes in a prediction-market format, artists are paid instantly, and value stays in the ecosystem rather than leaking to a middleman[2].

11 - What is ZABAL Games?

A three-month build-a-thon

A program that runs builders through workshops and shipping sprints to produce real products in the ecosystem - the bar is one small, shippable move, not a big build.

12 - What are the ZAO festivals?

ZAOstock, ZAOville, ZAO-PALOOZA, ZAO-CHELLA

The ecosystem's live events, run under a festivals umbrella - real-world gatherings that turn online community into an in-person network.

13 - How do I become a member?

Sign the Manifesto

Membership comes from signing the Manifesto - a short creed of five commitments. Signing it is an on-chain hat (via Hats Protocol, ERC-1155, soulbound and non-transferable, on Optimism)[6]. Read the full creed at thezao.xyz/papers/manifesto.

14 - What blockchain does The ZAO use?

Optimism, plus Base and Solana by lane

Respect governance tokens (the soulbound OG ERC-20 and the ZOR ERC-1155) live on Optimism. The broader ecosystem builds on other chains depending on the lane - WaveWarZ, for example, spans Solana and Base.
